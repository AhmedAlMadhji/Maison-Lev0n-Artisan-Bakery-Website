Our Story

A Bakery Built on Patience

Maison Lev0n began when Elias Levon opened a small atelier in Lyon with one stone deck oven and a belief that bread deserves time. Today, we carry that same philosophy — no shortcuts, no preservatives, no frozen dough.

Every loaf starts with flour milled within 120 miles. Our butter comes from heritage dairy farms. Fruit is picked at peak season. We bake in small batches so nothing sits on a shelf overnight.

  • Farm-to-oven ingredients Sourced locally, selected daily
  • Traditional recipes European techniques, refined over decades
  • Quality without compromise Limited quantities, maximum freshness

Daily Fresh Process

From Grain to Counter

Four steps we never skip — the reason every bite tastes the way it should.

  1. 01

    Select Ingredients

    Flour milled weekly, butter from heritage dairy, fruit at peak ripeness. We reject anything that doesn't meet our standard.

  2. 02

    Prepare Dough

    Natural starters rest 12 to 48 hours. Croissant dough is laminated by hand — fold by fold, layer by layer.

  3. 03

    Handcrafted Baking

    Stone deck ovens, rotated manually. Every loaf scored individually. Timing guided by experience, not timers alone.

  4. 04

    Fresh Daily Delivery

    On the counter by 7 AM. Local cafés and catering partners receive deliveries before 8 — never yesterday's bake.

Portfolio · Frontend Engineering

Project Transformation

From educational template to premium brand experience — a frontend case study.

Original State

A single-page HTML/CSS tutorial with Lorem ipsum copy, generic product cards, broken mobile navigation, missing assets, and no JavaScript functionality.

Problems Found

  • Non-functional mobile menu and order form
  • No brand identity or believable content
  • Font/CSS inconsistencies and accessibility gaps
  • Template-era parallax layout with weak visual hierarchy

Improvements Implemented

  • Full rebrand as Maison Lev0n with editorial layout system
  • 10 content sections + modular vanilla JavaScript
  • Product filtering, testimonial slider, form validation
  • SEO meta, Open Graph, JSON-LD, and WCAG-focused markup

Engineering Decisions

  • Vanilla JS modules — zero dependencies, fast load
  • CSS custom properties for scalable design tokens
  • Intersection Observer for scroll reveals and nav state
  • Semantic HTML with progressive enhancement

What I Learned

Premium frontend work is product design, not decoration. Restraint in animation, intentional typography, and honest content create trust. Building accessible, performant interfaces without frameworks demonstrates engineering maturity — and that's what recruiters look for in portfolio projects.
